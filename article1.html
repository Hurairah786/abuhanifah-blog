Is abandoning salah kufr?

Removing the confusion around a much discussed topic

Reflection

Published on 3 March 2026 • 4 min read

Is abandoning salah kufr?

According to the opinion of the majority of the scholars across the 3 madhahib, not praying due to laziness, whilst knowing that it's obligatory to pray, doesn't take you out of the fold of Islam.

Now in regards to the opinion held by Allamah Ibn Taymiyyah رحمه الله, and held by the likes of Shaykh Bin Baz and Shaykh Ibn Al Uthaymeen, then this is an opinion present in the the Hanbali Madhab only.

What is the meaning of the hadith then?

The Hadith many quote that says that between belief and disbelief is salah is to show the importance of praying, it doesn't necessarily mean that one who doesn't pray is a kafir.

Even if a person holds the Hanbali position on this topic, then there are still conditions, one of them being that the ruler or judge has to declare the person to be a kafir. Hence, if you seen someone who isn't praying, you can't just takfir them.

Nowadays uneducated TikTok scholars go around sharing Fiqh rulings without having understood them properly or even studied, hence we should ensure that we take our deen from reliable sources.

Does this mean it's fine not to pray?

Now this doesn't mean that it's acceptable to not pray - one who doesn't pray is committing a major sin and will be held accountable for it on Qiyamah, so if you're not praying, then make sure to repent and pray all of your salah.

How do I make up my salah?

If you’ve missed any salah in the past, then you must make up any missed prayers as soon as possible and estimate how many you've missed if you're unsure of an exact number. You only need to make up your fardh and witr , not your sunnah prayers.
